How to Never Miss an SSL Renewal Again

Published: November 18, 2025

68% of certificate outages are caused by forgotten renewals. Here are 5 ways to make expiry impossible.

1. Use This SSL Checker Daily

Bookmark our tool. Check all your domains in seconds. Set a calendar reminder — or better — automate it.

2. Let’s Encrypt + Certbot (Free & Automatic)

The gold standard: Certbot renews certificates automatically every 60–80 days. Works on Linux, Docker, Nginx, Apache, and more.

3. Free Monitoring Tools

  • UptimeRobot – Monitor port 443 + certificate expiry
  • SSL Labs Alerts – Email when cert nears expiry
  • Healthchecks.io – Run a cron job that pings our checker

4. Enterprise: Certificate Lifecycle Managers

Tools like AWS ACM, Google Certificate Manager, or Sectigo automate issuance and renewal across hundreds of domains.

5. The Nuclear Option: DNS CAA + Automation

Lock your domain to one CA using CAA records, then script renewal with ACME clients. Zero touch, zero risk.

FAQ

Can I get email alerts from this checker?

Not yet — but bookmark it and check weekly. We’re working on a free alert system.

Is 90-day renewal safe?

Yes — if automated. Google, Facebook, and Cloudflare all use 90-day certs.

The best renewal strategy is the one that runs without you.
