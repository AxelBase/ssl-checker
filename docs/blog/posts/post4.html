Let’s Encrypt vs Paid CAs

Let’s Encrypt vs Paid CAs: Which Should You Use?

Published: November 22, 2025

In 2025, over 60% of all websites use Let’s Encrypt. But paid CAs still dominate enterprise. So which one is right for you?

Let’s Encrypt: The Free Champion

Launched in 2016, Let’s Encrypt disrupted the industry by offering free, automated 90-day certificates. Today it secures more domains than any other CA — including Google, Shopify, and WordPress.com.

Paid CAs: Where They Still Win

  • Wildcard & EV Certificates — Let’s Encrypt only offers DV (domain-validated)
  • Support & Liability — Paid CAs offer 24/7 phone support and up to $1.75M warranty
  • Multi-year Plans — Buy 2–5 years at once (with re-issuance as needed)

When to Pay for a Certificate

You probably don’t need a paid cert if: - You run a blog, portfolio, or small business site - You can automate renewal - You don’t need wildcard (*.example.com)

You should pay if: - You need wildcard or EV certificates - You’re in a regulated industry (finance, healthcare) - Your legal team demands warranty coverage

FAQ

Is Let’s Encrypt less secure?

No. It uses the same encryption standards (RSA/ECC 256-bit+) as paid CAs.

Can I use Let’s Encrypt in production?

Yes — Netflix, Cisco, and the U.S. government do.

For 95% of websites in 2025, Let’s Encrypt + automation is the smart, secure, and free choice.
